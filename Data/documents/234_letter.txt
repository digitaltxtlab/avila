1. The concept that Paul has formed of Joseph's grandeur is most elevated. Nonetheless, there is a greater or less amount of good in the works we do for him, and we are not always aware of how upright our intention is. So it is necessary to proceed with caution as in all things and trust little in ourselves.
2. How he will laugh, mi padre, over all this foolishness, he who thinks he keeps this very much in mind. But with his other cares this could be forgotten, and it is good that I remind him; at least nothing is lost by my doing so.
